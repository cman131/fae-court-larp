# Hidden Heir — Court of Mirrors Storyline

*Storyteller reference. For the player-facing brief, see `characters/named/mab.md`.*

---

## Overview

Titania was the first and longest-reigning Queen of Fae Britain — older than the courts as they exist now, older than the current throne. After an unfortunate accident befell her (the details of which Mab does not discuss), Mab gathered the scattered courts and held the realm together for decades. She ruled quietly, invisibly, until Morgan arrived with three self-made Knights and the backing of enough of the fae to make fighting inadvisable.

What no one knew — what Mab has only recently confirmed — is that Titania left a line behind. Hidden, scattered, perhaps not even aware of itself. A descendant of the first queen. A bloodline claim that predates Morgan's throne, predates Mab's reign, predates every court compact currently in force.

That heir is here. At this Revel. Tonight.

**Mab's goal:** Find the heir, bind them to her, and use their claim as the foundation for her return to power. Not by force — by inevitability.

---

## Session Zero Setup

The storyteller must handle three things before the Revel begins:

1. **Assign the heir.** Designate one pool Sidhe or Sprite as the heir at session zero. Do not choose a Sovereign, Knight, or named character. Give that player the Heir mission card (M-28) below instead of a random draw. Do not tell Mab's player who it is.

2. **Assign the Watcher.** Designate a second pool Sidhe or Sprite as the Watcher. Give them mission card M-27. Tell the Watcher, privately, which guest they are protecting. Do not tell them anything else — not who hired the anonymous employer, not why.

3. **Brief Mab.** Tell Mab privately: (a) the Primordial Mirror shard has been stolen, (b) a clump of Beast Court hair was the only clue left behind, and (c) the shard glowed as she arrived in the capital, confirming the heir is among the Revel's guests. Do not tell her who the heir is.

**Dress the Mirror Alcove:** The Alcove needs a physical space in venue — a back room or curtained alcove dressed with mirrors, silver fabric, and something that reads as ancient court regalia. Prop the scrying mirror prominently. Leave a journal or book (Titania's household records — handwritten pages work well). The still basin can be a bowl of water with silver fabric draped around it.

## The Primordial Mirror Shard

The first scrying glass ever made was Titania's. She crafted it herself before any court existed, and fragments of it have passed through many hands since her fall. Mab acquired one such shard long ago — she will not say how.

The shard reacts to Titania's bloodline. When held near a true descendant, it grows warm, occasionally pulsing with a faint silver light. Mab has carried it for years and thought it dead. When she arrived in the capital, it stirred for the first time in decades.

The night before the Revel, the shard was stolen from her room. Only clue: a clump of coarse, dark hair caught in the carpet near the window. Beast Court fae hair is distinctive — coarse, with a faintly wild quality that Mirrors or Storms don't carry.

The shard is now in Oberon's possession. Mab does not know this yet.

---

## Beat 1 — The Stolen Shard (Act I: Arrivals)

**The task:** Mab examines the hair, or has a Mirror Sidhe do it. With Sight or Lore, the Beast Court origin is identifiable — the hair's quality is distinct. This points the investigation toward Spriggan's court.

**The complication:** Mab has a standing negative relationship with Spriggan (the ancient blood debt). She cannot simply approach him and ask questions without triggering a confrontation she doesn't want in Act I. She must work through her court or find indirect approaches into the Beasts.

**What Mab is doing:** Quietly cataloguing arrivals. Which Beast Court members are present? Is anyone absent? Does anyone seem nervous or avoidant? Her Mirror Sidhe are positioned to watch and report.

**Beat outcome:** Mab knows the theft came from the Beast Court. She enters Act II with a clear line of inquiry.

---

## Beat 2 — The Beast Court Thread (Act II: The Feast)

**The target NPC:** **Tannis**, a Beast Court Sprite. Small, rough-edged, the kind of fae who takes odd work for coin — not especially powerful, not especially loyal, the sort of creature compulsion is easy to use on. He was in the capital the night before the Revel. He is not here tonight.

**The task:** Mirror Sidhe circulate through Beast Court guests during the feast. The goal is indirect: compliment their court, trade small gossip, ask casually about "that friend of yours, the stocky one — where did he get to?" The Beasts who knew Tannis will either answer freely, grow curious themselves, or need a little nudging.

Information to extract: Tannis was in the capital. Tannis is not at the Revel. Tannis is staying at a specific inn or guesthouse nearby (the storyteller should pick one consistent with the venue).

**Complication — Spriggan:** If Spriggan notices Mirror fae paying particular attention to his court, he may confront Mab about the ancient blood debt. This is its own scene and can be productive drama — but it forces Mab to manage two problems simultaneously. The confrontation can also be a cover: if Mab is publicly sparring with Spriggan over the debt, no one thinks she's also quietly learning where Tannis sleeps.

**Beat outcome:** Mab has Tannis's location and the growing suspicion that he was told to do this. A Sprite his size doesn't steal from a Sovereign on his own initiative.

---

## Beat 3 — Tannis's Room (Act III: The Intrigue)

**The scene:** Mab leads a small group — her most trusted Mirror Sidhe, ideally her strong-positive — away from the Revel to Tannis's quarters. The room shows signs of someone hiding: curtains drawn, food uneaten, lock jammed from inside.

**The Fetch:** Guarding the room (or trapped inside with Tannis) is a **Fetch** — a mirrored, snarling copy of Tannis, set as a ward by Oberon's compulsion magic. The Fetch has Tannis's appearance, but its expression is blank, its movements slightly wrong, its eyes flat. It attacks anyone who enters. Defeating it requires physical conflict or abilities. When it is brought down, it dissolves without leaving a body.

*Storyteller note: The Fetch represents the weight of Oberon's compulsion on Tannis — a piece of the old king's will lodged in the room as a guardian. Its dissolution can be visually distinctive: it breaks apart like a reflection shattered in a mirror.*

**The real Tannis:** After the Fetch falls, the real Tannis is found — frightened, exhausted, cornered in the corner of the room. He didn't come to the Revel because he was terrified of what he'd done and more terrified of whoever sent him.

**The interrogation:** With Tannis subdued and the Fetch gone, Mab can question him. He breaks quickly — he is a small creature who got in over his head and is visibly relieved the decision has been taken out of his hands. Key information:

- Oberon approached him two nights before the Revel, when Tannis was alone in the market district. It did not feel like a request; it felt like a hand closing around his mind.
- He was told: *"Take the silver shard from Mab's room. Give it to me. Tell no one."*
- He passed the shard to Oberon in the early hours before dawn on the day of the Revel.
- He has no idea what the shard is, what it means, or why Oberon wanted it. He only knows Oberon's face and Oberon's voice.

**Beat outcome:** The shard is with Oberon. Mab now knows:
1. Oberon is aware of the heir and moved to intercept her before the Revel even started.
2. She needs to recover the shard from Oberon, or find an alternative path to confirm the heir's identity.

**Branching — shard recovery vs. working without it:**
Mab can pursue shard recovery from Oberon. This is legally grounded — he stole from her, and Barghest would back a formal accusation if given proof. But Tannis's word against Oberon's is fragile. She'll need either Tannis as a willing witness, or to negotiate with Oberon directly (he may return it in exchange for something, or use it as leverage). Alternatively, she can proceed to Beat 4 immediately and work from bloodline markers alone. In practice, Mab will likely pursue both paths simultaneously.

## Beat 4 — Lady Annis and the Bloodline Markers (Act III: The Intrigue)

**The NPC:** **Lady Annis**, an ancient Sprite who served in Titania's personal household for two centuries. She still lives, wandering from Revel to Revel as a living archive of the old courts. Tonight she sits in Oberon's orbit — he cultivates her company because she makes him look historically connected, like a Sovereign who remembers the old order. She is old, a little scattered, and speaks of the dead in present tense with perfect precision.

She does not know the heir exists. She simply knows what Titania's bloodline looks like.

**The task:** Mab or a Mirror Sidhe must get Annis alone — or at least away from Oberon's immediate attention — and ask careful questions about Titania's household, her children, her family, her gifts. Annis is not suspicious. She loves talking about the old days. The danger is that Oberon may be watching who takes an interest in her, and why.

**What Annis reveals** (if successfully approached and not rushed):

- Titania's descendants carry silver in their Glamour under pressure. It surfaces unbidden when they're stressed, joyful, or using their abilities at full stretch — a pale, almost white quality that is distinctly Titania's. They can't entirely suppress it.
- They have an instinctive sensitivity to hospitality law. They feel it bending before they can name why — an almost physical discomfort when oaths are bent or hospitality abused.
- They tend toward Sight even when their court tradition doesn't emphasize it.
- Almost as an afterthought, with the distant tone of someone remembering something very small: *"Her children always woke confused about whose crown it was."* They dream of a silver crown — not theirs exactly, but familiar.

**Beat outcome:** Mab has the bloodline markers. She can survey the assembled guests with new eyes. The shard can confirm; the markers let her build a shortlist without it.

---

## Beat 5 — The Mirror Alcove (Act IV: The Knights' Hour)

**The secret room:** Somewhere in the Revel space, hidden behind a glamour (a decorative panel that reads as wall, a passage the eye slides off), is the **Mirror Alcove** — Titania's private scrying chamber from when she hosted Revels at this location. Mab can sense it through her own mirror magic and Lore, even without the shard. The resonance is faint but there, like an old tune heard through a closed door.

**Room contents:**
- A large scrying mirror, framed in tarnished silver, etched with Titania's sigil along the frame
- A household journal in Titania's hand — family records, bloodline notes, names in an old fae script
- A wide basin of still water, draped in silver cloth — any descendant of Titania who looks into it will see, briefly, a silver crown reflected back onto their face before the surface stills

**The Watcher as obstacle:** When Mab has applied the bloodline markers and identified a prime suspect, she begins maneuvering them toward the Alcove. At this point the Watcher (the designated pool Sidhe) starts interfering — steering the suspect into other conversations, appearing at convenient moments, physically getting between Mab and her target. The Watcher doesn't know why Mab is interested; they only know that Mab circling their charge is wrong.

Mab must deal with the Watcher before getting the heir alone. Options:
- **Boggart Bargain** — offer something the Watcher genuinely needs
- **Leverage** — her Mirror Sidhe may have learned something useful about the Watcher during the event
- **Outmaneuver** — use the broken hospitality wards of Act IV, the chaos of Vortigern's rising influence, or simply split the Watcher away from the heir with a distraction

**The confirmation:** The heir stands before the still basin and looks into it. The silver crown appears — faint, momentary, unmistakable. Mab sees it. The heir may or may not see it themselves, depending on where they're looking.

**Beat outcome:** The heir is confirmed. Mab has certainty and proof. Act IV's broken hospitality wards give her more leverage than she had in earlier acts.

---

## Beat 6 — The Silver Bargain (Act IV/V)

**The approach:** Mab finds a private moment with the heir — still in the Alcove, or pulled aside at the edge of the Revel. She tells them what she knows. For the heir, this may be the first time they've heard any of it — but it explains things they've felt their whole life.

**What Mab offers:**
- Protection: no one else at this Revel will exploit the knowledge while Mab holds it
- Legitimacy: Titania's heir carries a bloodline claim older than Morgan's throne
- Partnership: together, they could reclaim something greater than either could alone

**What Mab wants:**
- Alliance — the heir's claim directed through her
- A sworn oath of some form
- Their silence about certain matters (namely, what happened to Titania)

**How the heir may respond:**

| Response | Mab's options |
|---|---|
| Accepts eagerly | The silver Glamour, the dreams, the sense of the old laws — it all clicks. Proceed to oath. |
| Accepts reluctantly | They see the logic but distrust Mab. A more modest first oath may open the door. |
| Demands proof | Bring them to the basin if not already done. The crown does not lie. |
| Refuses outright | Mab escalates — leverage, abilities (Hall of Mirrors, Boggart Bargain), exposure threat |
| Flees to another Sovereign | Mab must intercept, or control what the heir tells and to whom |

**Vortigern complication:** If Oberon has revealed himself as Vortigern by this point, the stakes are higher and the heir's value is obvious to everyone. A united Mab and Titania's heir facing Vortigern together is a stronger position than Mab alone — and the heir, if they understand what Vortigern is, has reason to want a powerful ally.

## NPCs

### Lady Annis

Ancient Sprite. Served in Titania's personal household for two centuries. Currently a wandering archivist of the old courts, a living record no library can replace. Oberon has cultivated her company for this Revel.

**Personality:** Old and occasionally scattered in her references, but brilliant when focused. Warm, genuinely fond of being asked about the past. Speaks of dead queens and dead children as if they just stepped out of the room. Not suspicious. Not dangerous. Not connected to any plot of her own.

**Role:** Source of bloodline markers in Beat 4. She does not know the heir exists. She should not learn why Mab was asking — if she does, she'll mention it to Oberon with complete innocence.

**Running Annis:** She can be run by the storyteller or assigned to a trusted player as an NPC role. She should be present in Oberon's social orbit during Acts I–II, accessible but requiring effort to reach in Act III.

---

### Tannis

Beast Court Sprite. Rough, small-time, takes odd work for coin or compulsion. Not cruel, not ambitious — just a fae who can be leaned on.

**What happened:** Oberon compelled him two nights before the Revel. Tannis had no meaningful ability to resist. He stole the shard, delivered it, and has been hiding in his room ever since, too frightened to attend the Revel and too frightened to run.

**Personality in the interrogation:** Relieved, almost. He's been alone with what he did for two days and he's exhausted by it. He will tell Mab everything he knows — which is not much beyond the compulsion, the instruction, and Oberon's face.

**Role:** Source of the Oberon connection in Beat 3. Does not know what the shard is or what the heir means. The storyteller runs this scene; Tannis can be a simple NPC or a brief assigned role.

## The Mirror Alcove (Physical Room Guide)

**Atmosphere:** The room should feel old and slightly out of time. It predates the current courts. Whatever decorating choices exist in the main Revel space, the Alcove should feel quieter, older, like something preserved in amber.

**Suggested dressing:**
- Mirrors (several, various sizes, tarnished frames)
- Silver or white fabric draped over surfaces
- Candles (real or LED)
- A large central mirror or a cloth-draped surface as the scrying focus
- A wide bowl of water (the still basin) on a low table or draped stand
- A prop journal or book with handwritten pages as Titania's household records

**The basin mechanic:** When the heir looks into the basin, the storyteller (or a second actor behind a mirror or screen) can produce a silver crown prop briefly above the basin's surface — or the storyteller can simply describe what Mab sees when the heir looks in, quietly, for Mab's player alone.

**Location:** The Alcove should be physically reachable from the main Revel space but require deliberate navigation — a side corridor, a service room, a curtained passage. It should not be obvious. The glamour hiding it can be represented by a simple sign on the door or a curtain across the entrance, with a quiet instruction to players: *this room is not visible unless you are told it is.*

## Mission Cards (New)

Add to `characters/secret-missions.md` as **M-27** and **M-28**.

### M-27 — The Watcher
*(Assigned by storyteller at session zero to a designated pool Sidhe or Sprite. The storyteller tells this player privately which guest they are protecting.)*

You were hired anonymously three months ago to attend this Revel and ensure the safety of a specific guest — someone you will recognize when you arrive. You do not know why this person needs protection, only that whoever hired you was ancient, deliberate, and very specific about your oath of silence. You are bound by that oath not to reveal their identity to anyone. If they seem to be in danger, act. Otherwise, watch and say nothing.

### M-28 — The Heir
*(Assigned by storyteller at session zero to a designated pool Sidhe or Sprite. Not for random draw.)*

You have always felt different — more attuned to the old ways than those around you. When you use Glamour under pressure, silver surfaces in it unbidden, no matter how you try to suppress it. You feel hospitality rules bending before you can name why. And every so often, you dream of a silver crown — not yours exactly, but familiar.

Tonight, more than ever, something feels different about you and about this Revel. You are here as [Name] of [Court] — but you have wondered, quietly, whether that is the whole story.

If someone approaches you tonight claiming to know the truth of your lineage, they may not be lying. What you do with that knowledge is yours to decide.
