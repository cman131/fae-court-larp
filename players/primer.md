# Player Primer: The Queen's Revel

*What you need to know before you arrive.*

---

## The World

The setting is a mythic Britain without mortals — a realm of fae courts, ancient pacts, and ironclad customs. The land is divided among three great city-courts, each ruled by a Sovereign. Beyond the cities, on the bleak outskirts of Fairy Country, lies the Autumn Forest — a place of refuge and slow death, where the dispossessed go when the courts are finished with them.

The Queen, **Morgan**, rules from the **City of Light**. She does not govern the city-courts directly; each Sovereign rules their own territory. But her word is law within the City of Light, and the Revel is her instrument: once a year, at summer's end, every sovereign power in Britain is expected to present themselves at her table, settle their debts in words rather than blood, and leave before dawn with any grudges formally acknowledged or formally set aside.

No one refuses the Revel. It is not a request.

---

## The Courts

**Court of Storms** — *Aurora's domain.* The high cliffs and cloud-veiled cities of the north. Radiant, charming, fond of music and the cutting word. They host the most beautiful revels in Britain and the most poisonous gossip.

**Court of Beasts** — *Woodwose's domain.* The deep wilds and mountain roots of the south. The oldest court present; Woodwose's people remember grievances measured in centuries. Blunt, powerful, visibly inhuman — horns, claws, fangs worn openly.

**Court of Mirrors** — *Mab's domain.* London and the trade-cities. Mab's clan thrives on secrets, bargains, and rumour. They wear human faces in mortal lands and carry every story Britain has to tell.

**The Autumn Forest** — *Oberon's domain.* Not a city. The Autumn Forest is where fae go when the courts are done with them — the cast-out, the broken, the powerless. Oberon shepherds them through the dying. He attends the Revel alone; his country has no one else to send.

**The Queen's Court** — *Morgan's seat.* Small and ceremonial; Morgan's authority is exercised through her three Knights. Tonight, with Morgan absent, the Knights stand in her place.

---

## Tonight

Some weeks ago, **Morgan** had a vision. She told no one what she saw. She sealed herself into the **Hawthorn Tower** and has not come out since. No one — not her Knights, not the arriving guests — knows what she saw.

The Revel came anyway. It always does.

Tonight's feast is hosted by the three Knights of the Queen's Court:

- **Barghest, Knight of the Sun** — direct, honourable, the Queen's blade; senior among the three
- **Mélusine, Knight of the Lake** — graceful, restless, the Queen's wing
- **Baobhan Sith, Knight of Song** — beguiling, lethal, the Queen's whisper

The Knights are oathbound to Morgan and to one another. They are not loyal to any city. They are hosting a feast their Queen is not attending, for guests who are watching her empty chair, and they do not yet know why it is empty.

---

## Why You Are Here

The Revel demands attendance from every sovereign power in Britain — whether you rule a court, serve a Knight, or owe someone a debt. You have come to the City of Light because custom commands it, because refusing would be noticed, and because there are accounts to settle: alliances to forge or fracture, favours to call in, and old wounds to air under the binding etiquette of a Queen's hospitality.

While Morgan is absent, her hospitality still holds. The Knights enforce it. Open violence is forbidden — but a formal duel, properly called, is a matter of etiquette, not a breach of it. In the City of Light, the distinction is enforced with real and serious weight.

What you do within those rules is up to you.

---

## Tone

This is a game of politics, not combat. Fae custom is ironclad, hospitality is sacred, and a well-placed word can destroy someone more thoroughly than a drawn blade. The mood is formal, ancient, and charged. The courts smile at one another and remember centuries of grievances.

Come with something you want. Come with something you owe. Come with something you are hiding. The Revel is where all of it surfaces.
