## Court of Storms

**Aurora's domain.** The high cliffs and cloud-veiled cities of the north. Aurora's people are radiant, charming, fond of music, dance, and the careful cruelty of a perfect smile. They host the most beautiful revels in Britain and the most poisonous gossip.

- *Themes:* beauty, illusion, revelry, song, the cutting word

---

## Court of Beasts

**Spriggan's domain.** The deep wilds and mountain roots of the south. Spriggan is the oldest sovereign present; her clan remembers grievances measured in centuries. They are blunt, dangerous, magically potent, and visibly inhuman — horns, claws, fangs worn openly.

- *Themes:* strength, ancestry, raw magic, the hunt, the long memory

---

## Court of Mirrors

**Mab's domain.** London and the trade-cities. Mab's clan thrives on secrets, identity, gossip, and bargains. They wear human faces in mortal lands and carry every story Britain has to tell. The court is the youngest and the most mercantile.

- *Themes:* secrets, masks, illusion, bargains, urban fae cunning

## The Autumn Forest

On the bleak outskirts of Fairy Country, the Autumn Forest is the country that no fae claims and to which no fae aspires. It is where the others go.

- Insect fairies with no real power, who could earn no place in the three courts.
- Small fairies — innocent, lightly minded, simple of speech — who could not navigate the intrigues that bind the three cities together.
- Fae driven out of their own clans by bullying, by shame, by an old wound no one wanted to heal.
- Fae accused — rightly or wrongly — of "spreading poison" through the cities, and cast out for it.

They come to the Autumn Forest. They die soon after. Oberon shepherds them through the dying.

His court is, in a sense, a court of the soon-to-be-dead. He brings none of them tonight; they could not endure the journey. He comes alone — the only voice the Autumn Forest can spare for a feast in Morgan's hall. The three city courts regard him with pity, contempt, or polite distance. Each of them has fae who fled to him and died there. None of them speak of it.

- *Themes:* refuge, mortality, mercy, the cost of the courts' polish, the moral weight a kindly sovereign can wield against those who looked away

## The Queen's Court

The City of Light is Morgan's seat. Her court is small and ceremonial. Tonight her chair stands empty; only her three sworn Knights are present to host the Revel in her name:

- **Barghest, Knight of the Sun** — direct, honorable, the Queen's blade. Senior among the three; presides in her absence.
- **Mélusine, Knight of the Lake** — graceful, restless, the Queen's wing
- **Baobhan Sith, Knight of Song** — beguiling, lethal, the Queen's whisper

The Knights are oathbound to Morgan and to one another. They are not loyal to any city. They do not yet know why their Queen has withdrawn.

## Oberon — the Public Account

Sovereign of the Autumn Forest. He brings no retinue — he could not, even if he wished to; his country is too sick, too small, too soon-dead. He answers to no other Sovereign. He has been, in public memory, a kindly shepherd of the dispossessed for as long as anyone in the three cities can remember.
