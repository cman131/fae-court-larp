# Court of Storms — The Gift

*Storyteller reference. For the player-facing brief, see `characters/named/aurora.md`.*

---

## Overview

Aurora has spent weeks preparing a grand public gift for this Revel — something that will make her, unambiguously, the most magnificent and beloved figure in the room. Her court has been accumulating wealth, favors, and debts in secret to fund it. The generosity is real. The extraction that funds it is hidden.

Three pieces remain to be collected at the Revel itself before Aurora considers the gift complete. And Aurora needs Morgan to witness the presentation — the Queen's adoration is the one thing she cannot manufacture herself. Morgan is not here. Aurora will not rest until that is corrected.

**Aurora's goal:** Complete the gift, present it in front of the assembled courts, and receive the Queen's adoration along with everyone else's. Find out why Morgan is absent. Get her out of that tower.

---

## Thread 1 — The Gift

The gift is mostly assembled. Three pieces remain to be collected tonight.

### Piece 1 — Tributes from the Rival Courts

Aurora wants a voluntary public contribution from each rival Sovereign court woven into the presentation — proof of cross-court unity and love. The givers should feel honored to be included.

**Mab (hard target):** Active enemy with a standing negative relationship. Aurora cannot approach this directly. Her court must find the angle — what does Mab want to be seen doing tonight? What tribute could be framed as Mab's idea rather than Aurora's ask?

**Spriggan:** A different kind of difficulty. Spriggan's pride is ancient and her court keeps its own counsel. A direct request reads as condescension. The approach must feel like recognition of the Beasts' standing, not a favor being extracted.

### Piece 2 — A Knight's Blessing

At least one of Morgan's Knights must formally endorse or bless the gift before it is presented. This anchors the gift in royal legitimacy — and ties Morgan symbolically to the moment even if she cannot be present in person.

This gives Aurora's court a natural reason to engage the Knights throughout Acts I and II. When the answers about Morgan come back evasive, that evasiveness feeds Thread 2.

Recommended targets: Baobhan Sith (most socially accessible) or Mélusine (already connected to Aurora's court by history). Barghest will ask hard questions about what exactly he is being asked to endorse.

### Piece 3 — The Centerpiece Object

A specific rare item held by a Revel attendee that Aurora has decided must be the heart of the presentation. Aurora's court acquires it through charm, trade, or social pressure. The owner may not fully understand what they are parting with.

*Storyteller note: Assign this item to a pool Sidhe or minor attendee at session zero. Choose something with physical presence — a jewel, a crafted object, a bound text, an instrument — that can be produced visually during the gift presentation.*

---

## Thread 2 — Where Is Morgan?

Aurora arrived expecting the Queen's presence. Its absence is a wound she will not show. Her court investigates throughout Acts I and II.

**What they are doing:** Pressing the Knights for information. Reading evasive answers. Watching who approaches the Hawthorn Tower and who comes back. Mapping the silences.

**What they learn, gradually:**
- The Knights are running the Revel by royal command — but the command came by written communication, not in person.
- No one has seen Morgan since the tower sealed.
- Baobhan Sith in particular is evasive and visibly tense whenever the tower is mentioned.

By Act III, Aurora's court has a location and a growing certainty that something is wrong.

---

## Thread 3 — The Hawthorn Tower

### The Approach

Aurora's court identifies Baobhan Sith as Morgan's adopted daughter — and the holder of a key to the tower. They bring her into a conversation: gentle, public, framed as loving concern. *Surely you want to check on her. She would want to attend. We can go together.*

Baobhan Sith will resist. She has reasons of her own to fear what is inside. Aurora's court must read the resistance correctly — this is not loyalty holding the door shut. This is dread.

The coercion works when Aurora's court gives Baobhan Sith a reason she can use: a framing, a witness, a permission she can tell herself she needed. Baobhan Sith's own suspicions about Oberon may lead her to open the door herself if Aurora's court offers to come with her.

### Inside the Tower

Signs of a struggle. Broken wards — the kind that resist from within, not without. Morgan is gone.

With Sight or Lore: the ward patterns are old and deliberate, the kind a queen sets to protect herself. They were broken from outside by something that knew their structure. This was not Morgan leaving. This was Morgan taken.

### After

Aurora's court now holds the secret Oberon walked into the Revel with. What they do with it is theirs to decide:
- Present the gift anyway — hollow, without the witness it was built for
- Bring the information to Barghest or the other Sovereigns
- Confront Oberon directly
- Hold it, and watch

## Storyteller Notes

**Session zero:** Assign the centerpiece object (Piece 3) to a specific attendee before the Revel. Brief that player on what they have and that someone may approach them about it tonight.

**Baobhan Sith coordination:** Her existing secret goal (Morgan told her to watch the King) already positions her for the tower scene. Do not tell her what Aurora's court will find — let the tower be the revelation for both of them simultaneously.

**Oberon timing:** Aurora's court reaching the tower and discovering Morgan's absence is independent of Vortigern's reveal. Either order works dramatically — if they find the tower before the reveal, they enter the reveal ahead of the room; if after, the discovery recontextualises everything they just witnessed.
