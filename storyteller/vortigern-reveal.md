# The Vortigern Reveal — Mechanics and Stagecraft

The reveal is the centerpiece of the evening; treat it like a magic trick, not a plot twist.

## Pre-Game Preparation

*(before session zero)*

- Cast Oberon privately, with a player you trust to hold a secret all evening and carry a heel turn cleanly.
- Give them both ability sheets (public and secret). Walk them through the reveal beat one-on-one.
- Brief them on **three plants** they should make during Acts I–III: a mocking toast in Act II, three contradictory private "truths" in Act III, and one favor publicly granted to a court that will become damning in Act IV.

*See also: [characters/named/oberon.md](../characters/named/oberon.md) for the player's full brief and stagecraft notes.*

---

## The Trigger

Don't let the reveal happen organically — pick a clear cue. Options:

- A storyteller rings a bell when the chaos in Act IV peaks.
- A specific Knight dies or breaks an oath.
- Barghest, in Morgan's name, asks Oberon to swear to defend the realm — and he refuses.

---

## The Moment

Oberon should physically change something visible: remove a mask, change a sash, lay down the crown, step onto a chair. The mechanical change (new sheet, voided oaths, Vortigern's Heart bonus) is announced aloud so everyone hears the new rules at once.

---

## After the Reveal

- Hospitality is **void**. All "by the Queen's peace" protections are gone.
- All oaths Oberon swore are **void** retroactively. Players who made bargains with him get nothing.
- He gains his Vortigern stat bonus and ability set.
- The remaining players have ~30 minutes of play to respond. Encourage them to negotiate among themselves rather than rush him — Vortigern is now mechanically the strongest character in the room and beating him by force is meant to be costly or impossible alone.

The good ending isn't "kill Vortigern." The good ending is "the courts learned to act as one in the time he gave them."
