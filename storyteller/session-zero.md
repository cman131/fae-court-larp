## Pre-Game Prep Checklist

- [ ] Cast Oberon privately, 1+ week ahead. Brief them on the secret sheet and the three plants.
- [ ] Write character sheets: stats, abilities, public goals, secret goals. One page per character.
- [ ] Print and cut the Secret Mission cards. One hat for the unnamed court members to draw from.
- [ ] Print and cut the Secret Knowledge entries (see `characters/secret-knowledge.md`). Distribute one per player before the mixer begins.
- [ ] Plan the Relationship Mixer (see below). Reserve 30 minutes between briefing and Act I. Identify a storyteller to monitor pick-distribution during phase 3.
- [ ] Print and distribute sheets at least 3 days before the event so players have time to think.
- [ ] Prepare the play space with at least three distinct "rooms" (great hall, private chamber, garden/balcony) for Act III intrigue.
- [ ] Choose a clear act-change signal (a bell, a horn, a song change).
- [ ] Prepare Oberon's costume swap so the reveal is visibly arresting.
- [ ] Recruit at least one Storyteller per 6 players to adjudicate conflicts.
- [ ] Hold a 20-minute rules walkthrough before Act I begins — practice one RPS conflict aloud.
- [ ] Plan a debrief: 15 minutes after the final tableau, sitting in a circle, in voice as players (not characters), to decompress and trade highlights.

## The Relationship Mixer — Overview

Before Act I begins, players need both a sense of who they are and a sense of who they know. The Relationship Mixer takes about thirty minutes and produces both. Run it after costumes, after the rules walkthrough, after the briefs are read in private — but **before** any in-character play begins.

### Phase 1 — Court Introductions

*(≈ 10 min)*

Group players physically by court:
- Storms in one corner with Aurora
- Beasts in another with Woodwose
- Mirrors in a third with Mab
- The Knights together (Barghest, Mélusine, Baobhan Sith) plus their personal names

Oberon stands a little apart, alone — as he should, all evening.

Each Sovereign briefly introduces their court to itself: who's who, who serves whom, what the court is publicly working toward tonight (the public goals — **not** the court storyline secret unless the Sovereign chooses to share). Each court member answers with their name, manner, and a one-line self-description. The Knights do the same among themselves.

### Phase 2 — The Mix

*(≈ 10 min)*

The storyteller forms randomized cross-court groups of three or four. Best if no two members of the same court share a group. Each group has roughly five minutes to introduce themselves to one another in character — name, court, one detail.

After five minutes, re-shuffle into fresh groups. By the end of phase 2, most players should have met most other players.

Oberon participates in the mix. This is his first chance to charm everyone in person; he should aim to spend at least a minute or two with everyone.

### Phase 3 — Relationship Selection

*(≈ 10 min)*

Each player picks three other players for relationships. Picks may come from anywhere in the room — not just from the mix-groups; the mixer was for exposure.

The three slots:
- **Strong positive** — trust, best-friend, love, admiration. The deepest bond.
- **Positive** — friend, respected peer, ally, mentor or mentee.
- **Negative** — rivalry, friction, hate, suspicion, old wound.

When you choose someone, walk over to them and propose the slot. The other player must agree before it locks. Together, the two of you write a one-line history on a card or in the brief sheet — *"best friends since we were both pages of Aurora"*, *"old rivals over a stolen mirror, never forgiven"*, *"I love them; they don't quite know."*

### Rules of the Mixer

- **Pre-set slots take priority.** Named characters arrive at the mixer with one or more slots already assigned by plot (Aurora and Mab fill each other's negative, Woodwose also fills Mab as her negative, Barghest and Mélusine fill each other's strong-positive, etc.). Those slots lock first. Fill remaining slots freely.
- **A player may be chosen by at most three others total.** This keeps Sovereigns and Knights from saturating every relationship card. If a player is already at three picks, pick someone else.
- **A player must be chosen by at least one other.** No one stands relationship-less. The storyteller monitors and nudges where needed.
- **Asymmetric relationships are allowed only by mutual agreement.** "I trust them; they're indifferent to me" can be powerful — but both players must consent.

### After the Mixer

By the end of phase 3, every player has:
- An assigned Secret Goal (named characters) or a drawn Secret Mission (court members)
- A Secret Knowledge entry (all players, including named characters)
- Three relationships, slotted as strong-positive / positive / negative
- A clear sense of who they know and how
- Five to ten minutes of in-character conversation already under their belt with most of the room

Act I can begin.
