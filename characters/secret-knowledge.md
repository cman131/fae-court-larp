## Setup Instructions

Every player — named characters, Sovereigns, Knights, and court members alike — receives one Secret Knowledge entry at session zero, distributed alongside their brief or Secret Mission card. Secret knowledge is a single piece of information your character holds privately. You know it as fact. You may share it, use it as leverage, or keep it hidden — the choice is yours.

**Storyteller setup:** Print this file. Cut along the dotted lines. Fold each entry. Distribute one per player before the mixer begins. Keep a private note of who received which entry — some entries connect, and you should know which threads are live at the table. If an entry clearly collides with a player's named brief in a way that feels unmanageable, quietly swap it.

Entries are intended to create collisions: a player who holds SK-01 and encounters Barghest talking confidently about the human army has a direct reason to engage. Not every entry will ignite — one live collision per entry is enough.

### SK-01
You were near the Autumn Forest three weeks ago. You heard the sounds of battle. When you crept close enough to see, there was no human army — only fae dead, and Barghest in the middle of them, her blade out, striking at things that were not there, or were not what she later claimed.

### SK-02
You know the Autumn lands. You know where Barghest was born. They are not the same country — not even close. Whatever she was defending three weeks ago, she was not at home. The geography of her story does not hold.

### SK-03
You have studied the deeper workings of Geis. At its second level, it does not merely compel — it replaces memory. A fae placed under it might recall an event that never happened with perfect clarity: sounds, faces, the full weight of conviction. They would not be lying. They would simply be wrong, and have no way to know it.

### SK-04
You spoke with the courier who delivered the sealed letter to Barghest three weeks ago. The letter's outer seal named the destination clearly: the Autumn Forest. Not a human settlement. Not Barghest's old country. The Autumn Forest. You are not sure what to do with this.

### SK-06
You know, or once heard, that Titania — the first Queen, the one before Mab — did not die in an accident. She chose to give up her immortality. There was a human. She loved him. She died of old age at his side, as mortals do. You are not sure who else knows this. It is not the kind of story people repeat at Revels.

. . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . .

### SK-07
Aurora's court gives lavishly — everyone has noticed. What fewer have noticed is the pattern of what it takes in return. Favors called precisely when they are most painful to give. Debts collected at exactly the moment they would cost the debtor most. The accumulation is too deliberate, the timing too knowing. Whatever Aurora is building toward, she has been doing it patiently for a very long time, and the scale of what she has quietly gathered is not merely the vanity of a Sovereign who likes to be admired.

. . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . .

### SK-08
Woodwose carries an oath older than the current courts — sworn to someone she calls only "a wandering king" whose name she cannot speak aloud, as though the oath has sealed even that from her. You heard her reference it once, unguarded. Fae oaths bind; old fae oaths bind differently — they do not merely compel, they alter. Whatever that king asked of her, the asking has not yet come due. Whatever the oath requires, she is bound to answer it, whether she wishes to or not.

. . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . . .

### SK-09
Mab was queen before Morgan. Most people treat this as history. Mab has not forgotten, and it is not history to her — it is interrupted. You have seen the pattern in her court's activities reaching back decades: steady, patient, laying groundwork in places no one looks. She is the only person at this Revel who was sovereign before the current order, and she has been positioning herself for a moment of crisis longer than anyone has been watching for it.

### SK-05

*(Assign to the player designated as the Autumn Forest survivor — see `storylines/court-of-beasts/index.md`, Thread 1, Act II.)*

You were in the Autumn Forest the night of the attack. You survived. You saw Barghest clearly. There were no humans. There was no invading army. There were the people who sheltered in that forest, and there was Barghest among them, and when it was over you ran. Whatever Barghest tells people tonight about what happened, you know it is not true — but you also know what a Knight of the Sun can do to someone who calls her a liar.
