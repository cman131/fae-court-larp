## History

You were the Queen of Fae Britain once. After Titania faded and the old order dissolved, it was Mab who gathered the courts and held the realm together — quietly, patiently, without drama. Your reign was long and largely invisible, which is precisely how you wanted it. A Queen who is noticed is a Queen who is contested.

Then Morgan appeared. She came with three knights she had made herself, with the backing of three courts who had grown tired of your careful hand on the wheel, and with a conviction about what the throne should look like that was very different from yours. You read the situation with the clarity that decades of intelligence work gives you: if you fought, the realm fractured. If you yielded, you survived.

You yielded. Gracefully, publicly, with a toast and a smile and not a trace of what you were actually thinking.

That was decades ago. You are Sovereign of the Court of Mirrors now — the same rank you held before the throne, the same rank you will hold after. You have been collecting, building, and waiting. You are in no hurry. You have held the throne once. You know exactly what it takes to hold it again.

## Secret Goal

You want the throne back.

Not through force — Morgan has three knights, and you don't, and besides, force is for fae who haven't thought far enough ahead. What you want is the right moment of crisis: the kind where legitimacy quietly cracks, the courts look for a steadying hand, and you are simply *there*, obvious and inevitable.

Morgan's disappearance tonight is the beginning of something. A Queen who seals herself into a tower and refuses to attend her own Revel is a Queen whose hold on the room is slipping, whether she knows it or not. You don't intend to make a move tonight — tonight is for watching, collecting, and positioning. Find out what the Knights know. Understand why the other Sovereigns are *really* here. Map the fracture lines.

When Morgan fails — or vanishes, or returns too changed to hold it together — you want to be the only name anyone can agree on.

## Court Storyline: The Hidden Heir

*Full storyteller reference: `storylines/court-of-mirrors/hidden-heir.md`*

Titania was the first Queen. The longest-reigning. The one who held the realm before there were courts to speak of. When she fell — through an unfortunate accident you prefer not to discuss — you gathered what remained and held it together. That was your claim to the throne: not blood, not conquest, but competence.

What you have only recently confirmed is that Titania left a line behind. Hidden, scattered, perhaps not even aware of itself. A descendant. A bloodline claim older than Morgan's throne, older than your reign, older than every court compact in force tonight.

That heir is here. At this Revel. You know this because you have carried a fragment of Titania's own scrying glass — the Primordial Mirror shard — for decades. When you arrived in the capital two days ago, it stirred for the first time in years. Warm. Pulsing. The heir is here.

**The shard was stolen from your room the night before the Revel.** You woke to find it gone. The only trace: a clump of coarse Beast Court hair caught in the carpet near the window.

You arrive at the Revel without your most important tool. Someone already moved against you before the night began.
