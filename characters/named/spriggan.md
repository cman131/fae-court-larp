## Persona and Appearance

**Faction:** Court of Beasts (your court).

**Rank:** Sovereign (27 stat points; 3 tokens).

Visibly inhuman. Horns, claws, antlers, a touch of fur or scale — wear at least one openly fae feature that the courtly Sidhe of other courts hide. Move slowly when others move quickly. Speak rarely and only with weight. You are the oldest of the three Sovereigns; let your silences be longer than your words.

## Secret Goal

Centuries ago you swore an oath to a wandering king whose face you can no longer recall. The Oberon at this Revel is no king — but something about him pulls at the memory in a way that has not happened in three hundred years. Find out, by Act IV, why your oath remembers him. And if the answer is what you have begun to fear, decide whether you keep the oath when it is called upon, or you break it for the first time in your long life.

## Relationships

**One slot pre-set: Mab (Negative).**

She owes you blood. She has owed it for a century. Walk over to Mab's player during the mixer and lock this in together — make the rivalry as cold and as ancient as you both want. (Aurora will also be locking Mab as her negative; Mab will end the mixer with two enemies, which is correct.)

**Strong positive and Positive slots: free.** Spriggan's deepest bonds are usually within her own court, so consider one of your Beast Sidhe as a strong positive. The positive could be one of the Knights (you respect honor) or another Sovereign (a quiet ally against Mab).

## Court Storyline: The Old Debt

A century ago — the storyteller will help you settle the specifics in session zero — Mab's Mirror Sidhe spilled the blood of one of your Beast Sidhe, by trickery rather than honest fight, and no recompense was paid. The Beast you lost was kin. The debt is owed to you by clan, and to the dead by name.

Tonight you collect, publicly, before the Queen's Knights. Mab will refuse, delay, or try to bargain her way out. You must make refusal impossible.

Direct your court members to:
- Block exits if Mab tries to leave the great hall before you've named the debt aloud.
- Bring witnesses — fae from other courts who knew the dead.
- Refuse all small bargains Mab offers your Sidhe; they will be designed to peel your support away one by one.
- Press Mab in private during Act III, so when you press her in public during Act IV she is already exhausted of options.

If you collect: triumph. If Mab pays a partial price that satisfies the Knights but not you: a wounded victory — your court remains owed, but the world thinks the matter closed. If Mab walks away with the debt unpaid: a generational humiliation. You may consider breaking hospitality. (Don't, before Act IV. After Act IV, the wards are down.)
