# The Queen's Revel — Character Briefs

This document contains:

- **Seven named-character briefs** (assigned in full): Oberon, the three Sovereigns (Aurora, Spriggan, Mab), and the three Knights (Barghest, Mélusine, Baobhan Sith).
- **Six template briefs** for the rest of the court members (Storm/Beast/Mirror Sidhe and Sprite). Players fill in their own fae name and draw one Secret Mission from the hat (see *Random_Draws.md*).

**Relationships are formed during the session-zero Relationship Mixer**, not assigned in advance. After the briefs are read, players group with their court for introductions, then mix into randomized cross-court groups, then each player picks three other players for relationships: one **strong positive** (trust, best-friend, love, admiration), one **positive** (friend, respected peer), and one **negative** (rivalry, friction, hate). Specifics are negotiated between the two players involved. See *Fae_Court_LARP_Design.md* ("Session Zero") for the full procedure.

For named characters, a few relationship slots are **pre-set** by the plot (Aurora ↔ Mab negative, Barghest ↔ Mélusine strong-positive, etc.). Those characters fill the remaining slots freely during the mixer.

Players who are *not* Oberon may choose their own personal name. The three Knights have a fixed Knight title (Barghest / Mélusine / Baobhan Sith) and a separate personal name of the player's choosing.

A note on the empty seat: **Morgan, the Queen, is not present.** Every reference to "the Queen" is to someone who chose to withdraw before her own Revel. The Knights speak in her name. Treat references to her with the gravity that absence demands.

## A. Named Characters (Assigned Briefs)

### 1. OBERON, the Wandering King *(Pre-cast — also Vortigern)*

> Read this brief privately. Do not share with other players. The storyteller will walk you through the reveal beat in person.

**Public name:** Oberon. **Do not let the player choose another name** — the name is part of the trick.

**Faction:** Sovereign of the **Autumn Forest**, the bleak country on the outskirts of Fairy Country where bullied fae, persecuted fae, fae accused of "spreading poison," and small powerless fae too innocent or simple for the cities' intrigues come to gather and die. He answers to no other Sovereign. He has come alone — the Autumn Forest can spare no other voice.

**Rank:** Sovereign (12 stat points). On reveal, gain +5 stat points distributed freely.

**Appearance & manner:** Worn-noble. Dress as a Sovereign but a poor one — autumn leaves at your brow rather than gold; a coat that has been mended; soft shoes that have walked far. Gentle in voice, careful in gesture, the manner of one used to comforting the dying. Smile only when it serves a kindness. Be especially attentive to Sprites and small fae at the Revel — your country is full of those who could not survive the cities, and you remember each one by name. The performance is a Sovereign who has carried too many funerals.

Bring two costume layers: the gentle autumn-shepherd one for Acts I–IV, and beneath it something sharper, colder, regal in an old and predatory way. The reveal is the moment they meet.

**Stats (public sheet, before reveal):**
- Glamour 6
- Iron 4
- Wyrd 2

**Abilities (public sheet — others see you use these):**
- **Sovereign's Mantle** — Once per scene, declare yourself the formal "host" of any conflict between two other parties, setting the stakes for both. (Your claim is the moral one: you answer to no other Sovereign and owe no city anything.)
- **Wanderer's Step** — Once per evening, enter any private scene uninvited; no one may refuse you entry. (You are the only voice your country has — none of the city courts will publicly turn away the lone Sovereign of the dying.)
- **Mantle of Mourning** — Pass any direct question by lowering your voice and naming a fae of theirs who died in your forest. You cannot be magically compelled to answer.

**Abilities (secret sheet — reveal only):**
- **Vortigern's Heart** — On reveal: gain +5 stat points distributed freely. All oaths Oberon swore are retroactively void.
- **Tyrant's Word** — Cannot be lied to for the remainder of the evening.
- **The Long Hunger** — Each character you defeat in a conflict after the reveal: take one of their unspent abilities to use yourself.

**Public goals (what other characters might think you want):**
1. Represent the Autumn Forest with the dignity its dead deserve. Be a careful, gentle guest in Morgan's hall.
2. Earn a private audience with each of the three Sovereigns before dawn. The pretext: to honor those of their courts who came to your forest and were laid to rest there.

**Secret goals (the real game):**
1. **In each private audience with a Sovereign**, name a specific fae of theirs who came to your forest and died in your care. (Have three names prepared before the Revel — work them out with the storyteller in advance. Each name should be plausible: a Sidhe gone too proud, a Sprite called "poison," an insect-fairy too small to seat.) The moral guilt is a wedge. Watch them squirm. Note who flinches; you will twist that one later.
2. **Plant three rumors during Act III**, one per Sovereign, each making them distrust one of the other two. (Suggested seeds — adapt as the night requires:
   - Tell Aurora that Mab has been spreading word that her court grows feeble.
   - Tell Spriggan that Aurora is bargaining with the Knights behind their back.
   - Tell Mab that Spriggan plans to claim the empty throne if Morgan does not return.)
3. **Provoke at least one Knight into compromising themselves** before Act IV ends — by oath broken, blood spilled in the great hall, or a public bargain with a faction.
4. **Reveal yourself as Vortigern at the storyteller's cue** and survive to dawn. The good outcome is not "kill everyone"; the good outcome is to leave the Revel with at least one Sovereign kneeling, one Knight dead, and the courts unable to unite against you.

**Relationships (Mixer):** In the session-zero mixer you participate strategically — your relationships are your levers. Coordinate with the storyteller, but the recommended slotting is:
- **Strong positive** → one of the three Sovereigns. This is the one you'll betray most directly during the reveal. Pick the one whose trust is most useful to obtain. (Aurora's vanity responds to gentleness; Mab's curiosity responds to a well-placed confidence. Spriggan's caution makes her the hardest target — but the highest payoff, because she is the only one whose ancient oath faintly remembers you.)
- **Positive** → a second Sovereign, or one of the Knights. The one you'll need a favor from in Act III.
- **Negative** → the third Sovereign, or Barghest. The rivalry you'll use to spark the conflict that compromises a Knight in Act IV.

Treat the mixer as your first plant — every kindness you forge tonight is one you will later betray. Be sincere as you forge them. The betrayal must surprise.

**Stagecraft notes:**
- Bring two costume layers. The outer one — gentle, autumn-shepherd, an autumn-leaf crown, a worn cloak. The inner one — sharper, colder, regal in an old and predatory way.
- The reveal moment: set the autumn-leaf crown at the foot of Morgan's empty chair (crush it underfoot if the mood holds you), shed the outer layer, open the secret ability sheet aloud, and deliver one short line prepared with the storyteller in advance.
- Until the cue, you are Oberon — kindly, careful, slightly tragic, attentive to every small fae in the room. **The reveal lands harder the more people genuinely cared about your country beforehand.** Make them mourn the kindly shepherd along with the discovery of what was beneath.

### 2. AURORA, Sovereign of the Court of Storms

**Personal name:** Aurora is your title and how others address you in court. You may choose a private name your closest companions use. Suggested elements: sky, dawn, song, cloud, north wind.

**Faction:** Court of Storms (your court).

**Rank:** Sovereign (12 stat points; 4 abilities; 3 tokens).

**Appearance & manner:** Tall, radiant, hair like cloud or sunrise. Move as though the air supports you. Speak warmly to inferiors and coolly to peers. Smile only when it costs the listener something.

**Stats (suggested):** Glamour 6, Iron 3, Wyrd 3.

**Abilities (suggested):**
- **Lure of the Revel** *(court)*
- **Song of Aurora** *(court)*
- **Glamour-Strike** *(universal)*
- **Iron Word** *(universal)*

**Public goals:**
1. Be acknowledged as the most beautiful and most magnificent of the three Sovereigns by night's end — through art, presence, and revelry.
2. Secure visible favor from at least one of the Queen's Knights before the Revel ends.

**Secret goal (assigned):** Long ago you stole a mirror from Mab — an object she would recognize on sight, and which she has been searching for ever since. You keep it in your possession. She must never learn it was you. If discovered, your court loses face at a moment when face is everything.

**Relationships (Mixer — one slot pre-set):**
- **Negative slot is pre-set: Mab.** She is your oldest rival and possibly your first lover. There is no fae in this room whose mind you read more easily, and none whose enmity costs you more. During the mixer, walk over to Mab's player and lock in the negative slot together. Negotiate the specifics with her — old lovers, old betrayal, professional rivalry, all of the above. (She will be coming to you to lock it in too; her brief says the same.)
- **Strong positive and Positive slots: free.** Pick them during the mixer based on the players you click with.

**COURT STORYLINE — "The Marriage Pact":**

You have been negotiating in quiet for months toward a single goal: bind **Mélusine, Knight of the Lake**, to one of your favored Storm Sidhe through marriage. This would tie the Queen's Court permanently to Storms — an alliance that outlasts even Morgan herself. Mélusine has not yet agreed.

Tonight you must:
- Arrange a private audience with Mélusine, ideally in Act III.
- Present a gift of significance — let your Sidhe choose what.
- Out-maneuver Mab, who suspects the pact and will try to spoil it. Either by counter-offer (extending her own marriage proposal to Mélusine) or by humiliation (revealing the gambit publicly to make refusal a face-saving move).
- Coordinate with your court members — direct one of your Sidhe to keep watch on Mab, another to flatter Mélusine through the first acts, and any Sprites to carry messages.

If you secure Mélusine's agreement before Act IV: triumph. If you secure her *consideration* and Mab is publicly humiliated for trying to interfere: also triumph. If you secure nothing and Mab walks away with Mélusine: ruin.

### 3. SPRIGGAN, Sovereign of the Court of Beasts

**Personal name:** Spriggan is your title. You may choose a private name — Beast court names are often hard, short, ancient: a single syllable, the name of a thing in the wood.

**Faction:** Court of Beasts (your court).

**Rank:** Sovereign (12 stat points; 4 abilities; 3 tokens).

**Appearance & manner:** Visibly inhuman. Horns, claws, antlers, a touch of fur or scale — wear at least one openly fae feature that the courtly Sidhe of other courts hide. Move slowly when others move quickly. Speak rarely and only with weight. You are the oldest of the three Sovereigns; let your silences be longer than your words.

**Stats (suggested):** Glamour 2, Iron 6, Wyrd 4.

**Abilities (suggested):**
- **Beast Form** *(court)*
- **Earth-Sense** *(court)*
- **Old Grudge** *(court)*
- **Iron Word** *(universal)*

**Public goals:**
1. Force Mab's court to repay, openly and in full, the old debt owed to your clan.
2. Receive a hunting blessing from the Queen's Knights for an upcoming campaign in contested wood.

**Secret goal (assigned):** Centuries ago you swore an oath to a wandering king whose face you can no longer recall. The Oberon at this Revel is no king — but something about him pulls at the memory in a way that has not happened in three hundred years. Find out, by Act IV, why your oath remembers him. And if the answer is what you have begun to fear, decide whether you keep the oath when it is called upon, or you break it for the first time in your long life.

**Relationships (Mixer — one slot pre-set):**
- **Negative slot is pre-set: Mab.** She owes you blood. She has owed it for a century. Walk over to Mab's player during the mixer and lock this in together — make the rivalry as cold and as ancient as you both want. (Aurora will also be locking Mab as her negative; Mab will end the mixer with two enemies, which is correct.)
- **Strong positive and Positive slots: free.** Spriggan's deepest bonds are usually within her own court, so consider one of your Beast Sidhe as a strong positive. The positive could be one of the Knights (you respect honor) or another Sovereign (a quiet ally against Mab).

**COURT STORYLINE — "The Old Debt":**

A century ago — the storyteller will help you settle the specifics in session zero — Mab's Mirror Sidhe spilled the blood of one of your Beast Sidhe, by trickery rather than honest fight, and no recompense was paid. The Beast you lost was kin. The debt is owed to you by clan, and to the dead by name.

Tonight you collect, publicly, before the Queen's Knights. Mab will refuse, delay, or try to bargain her way out. You must make refusal impossible.

Direct your court members to:
- Block exits if Mab tries to leave the great hall before you've named the debt aloud.
- Bring witnesses — fae from other courts who knew the dead.
- Refuse all small bargains Mab offers your Sidhe; they will be designed to peel your support away one by one.
- Press Mab in private during Act III, so when you press her in public during Act IV she is already exhausted of options.

If you collect: triumph. If Mab pays a partial price that satisfies the Knights but not you: a wounded victory — your court remains owed, but the world thinks the matter closed. If Mab walks away with the debt unpaid: a generational humiliation. You may consider breaking hospitality. (Don't, before Act IV. After Act IV, the wards are down.)

### 4. MAB, Sovereign of the Court of Mirrors

**Personal name:** Mab is your title. You may go by another, more personal name in private — Mirror court names lean toward urban, mercantile, modern things.

**Faction:** Court of Mirrors (your court).

**Rank:** Sovereign (12 stat points; 4 abilities; 3 tokens).

**Appearance & manner:** Refined, well-dressed, faintly mercurial. Your court is the youngest and the most mercantile; lean into that — a touch of human style alongside the fae. Speak quickly and pleasantly; laugh easily; never give away your second thought. You are the most well-informed person in the room and you must not look like it.

**Stats (suggested):** Glamour 4, Iron 2, Wyrd 6.

**Abilities (suggested):**
- **Maskwear** *(court)*
- **Boggart Bargain** *(court)*
- **Hall of Mirrors** *(court)*
- **Trueseeing** *(universal)*

**Public goals:**
1. Collect three new secrets tonight that you did not arrive with — at least one from each rival court.
2. Avoid, deflect, or partially settle Spriggan's claim on the old debt without paying its full price.

**Secret goal (assigned):** You already suspect Oberon is hiding something profound — the way his name moves in court gossip is *wrong*, in a way you've only ever sensed twice before in your long life. You do not know what he hides. Find out by Act IV, and decide what you'd do with the knowledge.

**Relationships (Mixer — one slot pre-set):**
- **Negative slot is pre-set: Aurora.** Your rival, your once-lover, the one who knows your secrets best of anyone here. You believe she stole a mirror from you a century ago. Walk over to Aurora's player during the mixer and lock this in together — negotiate the specifics. (Spriggan will also be locking you as her negative; you end the mixer as the most-hated woman in the room. This is correct.)
- **Strong positive and Positive slots: free.** Your court rarely forms strong bonds outside itself, but a strong-positive with one of your Mirror Sidhe gives you someone to trust with the Hidden Heir secret. The positive could be one of the Knights (you trade in their gossip) or even Oberon (a tempting and very dangerous choice).

**COURT STORYLINE — "The Hidden Heir":**

You know that one fae at the Revel tonight is secretly the unrecognized child of a long-dead king — a stolen child, a lost line, the kind of secret on which fae politics turns for a generation. *(The storyteller will tell you, privately, which character it is. Pick a Sidhe or Sprite role rather than a Sovereign.)*

You have not yet decided what to do with the knowledge. Three options:
1. **Expose the heir publicly** at the height of Act IV, for maximum chaos and maximum gossip credit.
2. **Sell the secret** to one of the other Sovereigns or to Oberon in exchange for a lasting advantage. (Be careful what you take from Oberon.)
3. **Bind the heir to you** privately — they discover you know, you offer to keep silent in exchange for a permanent oath of service.

Direct your court members to:
- Identify potential buyers for the secret without naming the secret itself.
- Gather corroborating proof — small details that would confirm the heir's parentage if pressed.
- Quietly intimidate the heir during Act III if you've chosen option 3 — make sure they understand silence is the price of survival.

The trap: if Oberon learns the heir's identity before you've moved, he will use them against you. Watch what he asks about.

### 5. BARGHEST, Knight of the Sun

**Personal name:** You are formally addressed as "Sir Barghest, Knight of the Sun." Choose a personal name that family or oath-siblings use. Examples: Lugh, Aedan, Cynric.

**Faction:** Queen's Court (the Knights). Senior among the three.

**Rank:** Sidhe (8 stat points), with the Knight's signature ability.

**Appearance & manner:** Tall, sun-coloured, in formal warrior dress or polished armor. Carry a visible blade. Honor-bound, direct, slow to anger and slower to forgive. You preside in Morgan's absence — every other person in the room knows your authority comes from her, and that her authority is, tonight, an empty chair.

**Stats (suggested):** Iron 4, Wyrd 3, Glamour 1.

**Abilities (4 total — 3 picks + Knight signature):**
- **Sun's Edge** *(Knight signature)*
- **Iron Word** *(universal)*
- **Trueseeing** *(universal)*
- **Hearth's Mercy** *(universal)*

**Public goals:**
1. Maintain the Queen's peace until dawn. Hospitality is binding by her standing word, whether or not she is here.
2. If any open grievance is raised between courts, resolve it before blood is spilled. You have the authority to compel a settlement. Use it sparingly — once you compel a Sovereign, you cannot do it twice in one night.

**Secret goal (assigned):** You suspect Oberon. You have no proof, only a feeling you've been carrying since the third time you saw him this season — something in the way he greets Morgan. You may not know it consciously, but you have already begun watching him. Find proof tonight, or decide whether a Knight of the Sun may act on suspicion alone.

**Relationships (Mixer — one slot pre-set):**
- **Strong positive slot is pre-set: Mélusine.** Your oath-sister. You would die for her without hesitation. During the mixer, walk over to her player and lock the slot in together — agree on how long you have been sworn, what you have survived together, what your private name for one another is. You also know — though you have not let yourself name it — that her oath to Morgan has begun to fray. Do not raise this with her player at the mixer; let the strain emerge during play.
- **Positive and Negative slots: free.** Recommended negative: Oberon, given your suspicions. The Knight signature ability Sun's Edge means a hostile relationship with the King will be felt across the table.

**Stagecraft notes:** In Act II, you give the welcome speech in Morgan's name. Prepare a few lines in advance — the welcome should be warm, formal, and end with a brief moment of silence "for the seat that remains empty tonight." That silence is the tone the whole evening lives in.

### 6. MÉLUSINE, Knight of the Lake

**Personal name:** Formally "Lady Mélusine, Knight of the Lake." Choose a personal name. Examples: Niamh, Avalon, Sionán.

**Faction:** Queen's Court (the Knights).

**Rank:** Sidhe (8 stat points), with the Knight's signature ability.

**Appearance & manner:** Graceful, water-colored — silver, slate, pale blue. Light armor or flowing courtly attire; a long blade or spear at your hip. Restless by nature; never quite at rest in any one company. You are the Knight most likely to be courted by the other courts, and the most likely to entertain the courting.

**Stats (suggested):** Iron 3, Glamour 3, Wyrd 2.

**Abilities:**
- **Lake's Veil** *(Knight signature)*
- **Whispered Tongue** *(universal)*
- **Hearth's Mercy** *(universal)*
- **Maskwear** *(Mirror court — unusual for a Knight, suggests your wandering nature)*

**Public goals:**
1. Keep the courts in motion — no single court should monopolize the Knights' attention or favor tonight. Move among them.
2. Honor the Queen's hospitality in spirit as well as letter.

**Secret goal (assigned):** You have been offered, separately, a marriage pact by Aurora (Court of Storms) and a counter-offer by Mab (Court of Mirrors). The third option is to remain free, sworn only to Morgan. Decide before the Revel ends — and reckon with what your refusal of two costs the third.

**Relationships (Mixer — one slot pre-set):**
- **Strong positive slot is pre-set: Barghest.** Your oath-sibling, who would not survive your betrayal of the Knights. Lock the slot in with his player at the mixer — agree on the shared history. He has already begun to suspect your wavering. Do not raise that with his player at the mixer; let it bleed in during play.
- **Positive and Negative slots: free.** The natural negative is whoever you'll ultimately disappoint — perhaps Aurora or Mab, whichever marriage offer you intend to refuse. Or Oberon, if your instincts about him sharpen. Choose during the mixer; you'll know who feels right when you talk to them.

### 7. BAOBHAN SITH, Knight of Song

**Personal name:** Formally "Lady Baobhan Sith, Knight of Song." Choose a personal name. Examples: Branwen, Carys, Eilan.

**Faction:** Queen's Court (the Knights).

**Rank:** Sidhe (8 stat points), with the Knight's signature ability.

**Appearance & manner:** Strikingly beautiful, music-coded — instruments, jewelry of bells or strings, fine courtly dress with one or two hidden blades. Charming to a calculated degree; sing or hum quietly as you move. The Knight most likely to be underestimated.

**Stats (suggested):** Glamour 4, Wyrd 3, Iron 1.

**Abilities:**
- **Wind's Lament** *(Knight signature)*
- **Trueseeing** *(universal)*
- **Glamour-Strike** *(universal)*
- **Whispered Tongue** *(universal)*

**Public goals:**
1. Identify any guest tonight acting in bad faith and quietly report it to Barghest.
2. Entertain the Revel — your charm is part of the Queen's reach. Sing at least once in public, dance with at least three different guests.

**Secret goal (assigned):** You are Morgan's private informant. Three months ago, before she sealed herself in the Hawthorn Tower, she told you in private: *"Watch the King."* You have watched. You have seen things you cannot yet make sense of. Tonight you decide whether to act on what you've seen, and whether to trust your fellow Knights with the burden before Oberon makes the choice for you.

**Relationships (Mixer — one slot guided):**
- **Negative slot, guided:** During the mixer, the player you have been quietly studying on Morgan's orders fills your negative slot. Pick them in soft consultation with the storyteller — Oberon is the obvious candidate, but a non-obvious choice (a Mirror Sidhe, perhaps) can produce a more interesting plot. They do not know you have been watching. When you lock the slot in with their player, present it as a mundane friction (a slight, an old disagreement) — let the truth come out during play.
- **Strong positive and Positive slots: free.** A strong positive with one of your fellow Knights gives you somewhere to bring your worries when the time comes.

## C. Storyteller Notes

- **Court sizes:** For a 12-player cast: Oberon (1) + 3 Sovereigns + 3 Knights + 2 Sidhe per court (6) = 13. Drop one Sprite or shrink a court by a Sidhe for 12. For 8–10: drop Sprites; each Sovereign brings one Sidhe.
- **Court coordination:** Hand the three Sovereigns their court storylines at session zero. Encourage them to spend the first 20 minutes there briefing their court members (without revealing their secret goal) — this is how court members develop a sense of what their public goals truly serve.
- **The Hidden Heir:** When Mab's brief is handed out, also tell Mab — privately — which Sidhe or Sprite at the table is the unrecognized heir. Do **not** tell that Sidhe/Sprite. They should not know.
- **Cross-plot interactions you can lean on:**
  - The Marriage Pact (Aurora) and The Hidden Heir (Mab) intersect if Mab's chosen heir is in Aurora's court. Consider this.
  - The Old Debt (Spriggan) gives Oberon an obvious lever during his Act III plant phase — tell Spriggan privately that Mab plans to escape the debt entirely, even if it isn't true.
  - Mélusine's secret goal (three offers) is one of the most flexible pieces in the room. Use it to drive Acts III–IV pressure.
- **Re-draws:** If a Sprite or Sidhe draws a Secret Mission that the storyteller knows will collide ruinously with a Sovereign plot, allow a re-draw quietly. Better re-draws than dead plots.
- **Mixer monitoring:** During the Relationship Mixer's phase 3, watch for clustering. If Sovereigns and Knights are filling everyone's cards, nudge players toward Sprites and Sidhe so no one ends the mixer relationship-less.
