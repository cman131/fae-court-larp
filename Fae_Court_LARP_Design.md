# The Queen's Revel
### A Celtic Fae LARP — Design Document

*A one-evening live-action drama of fae politics, secret bargains, and ancient betrayal, structurally adapted from Vampire: The Masquerade LARP and thematically inspired by the world of Avalon le Fae.*

---

## 1. Concept

The Fae Queen **Morgan** has summoned the rulers of Britain's three great courts to a Revel at her seat in the City of Light — and then sealed herself into the Hawthorn Tower and refused to come out. Her three Knights host the Revel in her stead.

The pretext is celebration. The reality is politics — debts to settle, alliances to forge, and old grudges to air under the binding etiquette of a Queen's hospitality, enforced by Knights who do not yet know what their Queen saw before she withdrew.

Among the guests is **Oberon**, Sovereign of the Autumn Forest — that bleak country on the outskirts of Fairy Country where the broken, the bullied, and the powerless go to die. He answers to no other Sovereign. He has come alone; the Autumn Forest has no other voice to send.

By dawn, the Knights will be drawn into bloodshed, the courts will be at one another's throats, and Oberon will have revealed himself as **Vortigern**, the ancient calamity wearing a kindly Sovereign's face.

The players don't know any of that. Most of them, anyway.

---

## 2. The World

The setting is a mythic Britain stripped of mortals — a realm of fae courts, ancient pacts, and ironclad customs. The Queen rules in name from her seat at the **City of Light**, but in practice each Fae Clan rules its own territory, and the Queen's writ extends only as far as the Knights can ride.

The throne has stood since memory, and the Queen — **Morgan** — is its current occupant. Her authority is exercised lightly but absolutely; she keeps the peace by hosting feasts where the courts can settle scores with words instead of war.

Tonight, she is not at her own feast. Some weeks ago Morgan had a vision. She told no one what she saw. The next morning she announced the Revel, sealed herself into the Hawthorn Tower, and would not come out. The Knights have been left to host. They do not know what Morgan saw. Neither does anyone else.

Oberon, Sovereign of the Autumn Forest, has come at Morgan's invitation. He answers to no other Sovereign. He travels rarely, and only when the dignity of his small, dying country demands it. He has come to the Revel; Morgan has not.

### The Three Cities (Player Courts)

**The Court of Storms — Aurora's domain**
The high cliffs and cloud-veiled cities of the north. Aurora's people are radiant, charming, fond of music, dance, and the careful cruelty of a perfect smile. They host the most beautiful revels in Britain and the most poisonous gossip.
- *Themes:* beauty, illusion, revelry, song, the cutting word

**The Court of Beasts — Spriggan's domain**
The deep wilds and mountain roots of the south. Spriggan is the oldest sovereign present; her clan remembers grievances measured in centuries. They are blunt, dangerous, magically potent, and visibly inhuman — horns, claws, fangs worn openly.
- *Themes:* strength, ancestry, raw magic, the hunt, the long memory

**The Court of Mirrors — Mab's domain**
London and the trade-cities. Mab's clan thrives on secrets, identity, gossip, and bargains. They wear human faces in mortal lands and carry every story Britain has to tell. The court is the youngest and the most mercantile.
- *Themes:* secrets, masks, illusion, bargains, urban fae cunning

### The Autumn Forest — Oberon's domain

On the bleak outskirts of Fairy Country, the Autumn Forest is the country that no fae claims and to which no fae aspires. It is where the others go.

- Insect fairies with no real power, who could earn no place in the three courts.
- Small fairies — innocent, lightly minded, simple of speech — who could not navigate the intrigues that bind the three cities together.
- Fae driven out of their own clans by bullying, by shame, by an old wound no one wanted to heal.
- Fae accused — rightly or wrongly — of "spreading poison" through the cities, and cast out for it.

They come to the Autumn Forest. They die soon after. Oberon shepherds them through the dying.

His court is, in a sense, a court of the soon-to-be-dead. He brings none of them tonight; they could not endure the journey. He comes alone — the only voice the Autumn Forest can spare for a feast in Morgan's hall. The three city courts regard him with pity, contempt, or polite distance. Each of them has fae who fled to him and died there. None of them speak of it.

- *Themes:* refuge, mortality, mercy, the cost of the courts' polish, the moral weight a kindly sovereign can wield against those who looked away

### The Queen's Court (Knight Faction)

The City of Light is Morgan's seat. Her court is small and ceremonial. Tonight her chair stands empty; only her three sworn Knights are present to host the Revel in her name:
- **Barghest, Knight of the Sun** — direct, honorable, the Queen's blade. Senior among the three; presides in her absence.
- **Mélusine, Knight of the Lake** — graceful, restless, the Queen's wing
- **Baobhan Sith, Knight of Song** — beguiling, lethal, the Queen's whisper

The Knights are oathbound to Morgan and to one another. They are not loyal to any city. They do not yet know why their Queen has withdrawn.

### Oberon

Sovereign of the Autumn Forest. He brings no retinue — he could not, even if he wished to; his country is too sick, too small, too soon-dead. He answers to no other Sovereign. He has been, in public memory, a kindly shepherd of the dispossessed for as long as anyone in the three cities can remember.

He is also, secretly, **Vortigern** — an ancient calamity who wears a kindly Sovereign's face. The Autumn Forest is his sink. The fae who flee to him for refuge die in his care because he has been feeding on those deaths for centuries; the country's purpose, designed long ago by his own hand, was always to draw the weak in so he could grow stronger off their endings. The three city courts are unknowingly complicit — every shamed Sidhe they cast out, every accused fae they exile, every small one they will not seat, walks to his forest.

His goal at the Revel is to use the courts' contempt for his country against them. To set them at each other's throats over old guilts. To provoke the Knights into compromising themselves. To stand quietly, kindly, as the moral weight in the room — and when no one is left strong enough to oppose him, to shed the Oberon mask.

## 3. Renamed Rank System

Replace Leader / Noble / Commoner with fae-flavored equivalents. Three good options — pick the set that sounds best to you:

| Tier | Primary (recommended) | Alt 1 | Alt 2 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Leader | **Sovereign** | Crowned | Liege |
| Noble | **Sidhe** | Highborn | Trooping Fae |
| Commoner | **Sprite** | Hob | Solitary Fae |

Below I'll use Sovereign / Sidhe / Sprite.

### Point allocations by rank

| Rank | Stat Points | Abilities | Tokens (items / favors) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sovereign | 12 (max 6 in one stat) | 4 | 3 |
| Sidhe | 8 (max 4) | 3 | 2 |
| Sprite | 5 (max 3) | 2 | 1 |

Knights are built on the Sidhe chassis with one bonus ability (the Knight's signature). Oberon is built on the Sovereign chassis with one *secret* additional ability sheet for the reveal.

## 4. Stats

Three stats, each rated 1–6. Every fae has all three.

- **Glamour** — beauty, charm, illusion, social pull, persuasion
- **Iron** — physical might, willpower, combat, endurance
- **Wyrd** — cunning, magic, perception, secrets, riddles

When a conflict is declared, the challenger names the stat that applies. Both parties bid stat points (see §6).

---

## 5. Abilities

Abilities are one-shot or once-per-scene cards that bend the rules. Players choose them at character creation from two pools: **Universal** and **Court**.

### Universal abilities (anyone may take)

- **Glamour-Strike** — Once per scene, force the opponent to lose an RPS challenge without throwing. Stat-bid still happens.
- **Iron Word** — When you swear an oath aloud, you cannot break it without a Wyrd 5+ conflict; in return, you cannot be magically compelled this scene.
- **Whispered Tongue** — Pass a short message silently to one person you can see.
- **Hearth's Mercy** — Once per evening, restore one spent ability or stat point.
- **Trueseeing** — Once per evening, demand a yes/no question be answered honestly.

### Court abilities

**Storms (Aurora's court)**
- **Lure of the Revel** *(Glamour)* — Draw any one character into a private conversation; they cannot disengage for 3 minutes without spending a Glamour point.
- **Stormwalk** *(Iron)* — Pass any one social "barrier" (a closed circle, a forbidden room) once without challenge.
- **Song of Aurora** *(Wyrd)* — While you sing, dance, or perform, any character within earshot must answer one personal question truthfully when you stop.

**Beasts (Spriggan's court)**
- **Beast Form** *(Iron)* — When you would lose a physical conflict, force a tie instead. Once per evening.
- **Earth-Sense** *(Wyrd)* — Detect when anyone in your line of sight is lying about a location, a movement, or a meeting.
- **Old Grudge** *(Glamour)* — Publicly name a wrong done to your clan. The accused must respond honestly or pay a Glamour point to deflect.

**Mirrors (Mab's court)**
- **Maskwear** *(Glamour)* — Briefly impersonate another character's voice or manner. Convince one character of one small lie.
- **Boggart Bargain** *(Wyrd)* — Offer a secret-for-secret trade. If accepted, both parties must trade truthfully.
- **Hall of Mirrors** *(Iron)* — When attacked, redirect the attack to another character within 3 paces. Once per evening.

### Knight signature abilities

- **Sun's Edge** *(Barghest)* — Win one Iron conflict without RPS, once per evening.
- **Lake's Veil** *(Mélusine)* — Vanish from a scene for up to 5 real minutes; return anywhere in the play space.
- **Wind's Lament** *(Baobhan Sith)* — Compel one direct, truthful answer to one question, once per evening.

### Oberon's abilities

**Public sheet (what other players see him use)**
- **Sovereign's Mantle** — May declare himself the formal "host" of any conflict, setting the stakes for both sides. (Oberon's claim is the moral one: he answers to no other Sovereign and owes no city anything.)
- **Wanderer's Step** — Enter any private scene uninvited once per evening; no one may refuse him entry.
- **Crown of Idleness** — Pass any direct question by saying "I have no opinion on this." Cannot be compelled to answer.

**Secret sheet (revealed when Oberon becomes Vortigern — see §8)**
- **Vortigern's Heart** — On reveal, gain 5 additional stat points distributed freely. All prior oaths Oberon swore are void.
- **Tyrant's Word** — Cannot be lied to for the remainder of the evening.
- **The Long Hunger** — Each character you defeat in a conflict after the reveal gives you one of their unspent abilities to use yourself.

## 6. Conflict Resolution (No Dice)

When two characters disagree on an outcome:

1. The challenger names a **stat** (Glamour / Iron / Wyrd).
2. Each party may **bid** any number of points from that stat. Bids are simultaneous and revealed together. Bid points are spent for the rest of the scene whether you win or lose.
3. Both players throw **rock-paper-scissors**.
4. **Winner of RPS wins the conflict.** On a tie, the higher bid wins. On equal bids and a tie, the higher *unspent* stat wins. If still tied, throw again.
5. Either player may spend an ability before or after the throw, where the ability text allows.

Stat points refresh between **acts** (not scenes). This forces players to ration.

For multi-party conflicts (e.g., a duel witnessed by court), a Storyteller adjudicates whose stat counts and may award narrative bonuses for good roleplay.

## 8. Story Structure — Five Acts (≈ 4 hours)

### Act I — Arrivals (35–45 min)
The Knights welcome each court at the gate, speaking in the Queen's name. Public introductions, gifts presented, initial impressions. The empty seat at the high table is the first thing every guest notices. Players establish first scenes and pursue light goals.
- **Storyteller beat:** Oberon arrives last, alone — as he must; the Autumn Forest can spare no other voice. He greets each Sovereign warmly. He asks after the Queen, knowing she will not come. He thanks the Knights for receiving him as a peer — this is not always granted to one who rules over dying fae.
- **Conflict tone:** posturing only, no violence permitted by the Queen's standing hospitality.

### Act II — The Feast (45–60 min)
The empty chair presides over a formal banquet. Barghest sits at its right hand and gives the welcome in Morgan's name. Toasts are made — and toasts are binding by the Queen's standing word, whether or not she is here to witness them. This is the act for public goals, declared alliances, and open challenges.
- **Storyteller beat:** Oberon proposes a toast that subtly mocks each court, gauging reactions.
- **Conflict tone:** social conflicts encouraged; physical conflicts still forbidden by hospitality.

### Act III — The Intrigue (45–60 min)
After the feast, the courts disperse to private chambers, gardens, the balconies. **This is the act for secret meetings.** Plots accelerate. Most ability use should happen here.
- **Storyteller beat:** Oberon meets privately with at least three different characters and tells each one a different "truth" about another character. Plant the rumors that fuel Act IV.
- **Conflict tone:** anything goes short of open violence; betrayals begin.

### Act IV — The Knights' Hour (30–45 min)
Barghest calls the courts back before the empty throne. A formal grievance is aired — staged by Oberon's manipulations. **The Knights are forced to choose a side** in the Queen's absence, and at least one Knight will end the act compromised, dead, or sworn to the wrong cause.
- **Storyteller beat:** The hospitality wards break. Physical conflict is now legal.
- **Conflict tone:** open. Bodies may fall.

### Act V — The Reveal & Resolution (30–45 min)
At a moment of maximum chaos, the storyteller cues Oberon. He sets down his autumn-leaf crown at the foot of Morgan's empty chair, opens his secret ability sheet, and **declares himself Vortigern.** Hospitality is voided. Old oaths are dust. Morgan does not emerge from the Hawthorn Tower. The remaining cast must decide — together — how Britain meets him: united, fractured, or kneeling.

End the evening on a tableau, not a winner. Score and debrief afterward.

## 9. The Vortigern Reveal — Mechanics & Stagecraft

The reveal is the centerpiece of the evening; treat it like a magic trick, not a plot twist.

**Pre-game (before session zero)**
- Cast Oberon privately, with a player you trust to hold a secret all evening and carry a heel turn cleanly.
- Give them both ability sheets (public and secret). Walk them through the reveal beat one-on-one.
- Brief them on **three plants** they should make during Acts I–III: a mocking toast in Act II, three contradictory private "truths" in Act III, and one favor publicly granted to a court that will become damning in Act IV.

**The trigger**
Don't let the reveal happen organically — pick a clear cue. Options:
- A storyteller rings a bell when the chaos in Act IV peaks.
- A specific Knight dies or breaks an oath.
- Barghest, in Morgan's name, asks Oberon to swear to defend the realm — and he refuses.

**The moment**
Oberon should physically change something visible: remove a mask, change a sash, lay down the crown, step onto a chair. The mechanical change (new sheet, voided oaths, Vortigern's Heart bonus) is announced aloud so everyone hears the new rules at once.

**After the reveal**
- Hospitality is **void**. All "by the Queen's peace" protections are gone.
- All oaths Oberon swore are **void** retroactively. Players who made bargains with him get nothing.
- He gains his Vortigern stat bonus and ability set.
- The remaining players have ~30 minutes of play to respond. Encourage them to negotiate among themselves rather than rush him — Vortigern is now mechanically the strongest character in the room and beating him by force is meant to be costly or impossible alone.

The good ending isn't "kill Vortigern." The good ending is "the courts learned to act as one in the time he gave them."

## 10. Faction & Character Goals

Each player should be given **2 public goals** and **2 secret goals** at session zero. Examples:

**Aurora (Storms, Sovereign)**
- *Public:* Be seen as the most beautiful of the sovereigns by the end of the night.
- *Public:* Secure a marriage-pact between your court and one Knight.
- *Secret:* You stole a relic from Mab a century ago. She must never know.
- *Secret:* You believe Oberon is in love with you. Confirm it.

**Spriggan (Beasts, Sovereign)**
- *Public:* Force Mab to repay an ancient debt before the night ends.
- *Public:* Get the Queen's blessing for a hunt on contested land.
- *Secret:* Long ago you swore an oath to a wandering king whose face you can no longer recall. The Oberon at the Revel is no king — but something about him pulls at the memory. Find out why.
- *Secret:* If the Queen falls, you intend to claim her throne.

**Mab (Mirrors, Sovereign)**
- *Public:* Collect three new secrets you didn't have at the start of the night.
- *Public:* Avoid paying Spriggan back.
- *Secret:* You already know Oberon is hiding something. You don't know what.
- *Secret:* You have an informant in another court. Reveal them — to your benefit — at the right moment.

**Barghest (Knight of the Sun)**
- *Public:* Maintain the Queen's peace until dawn.
- *Public:* Resolve the grievance between Storms and Mirrors before blood is spilled.
- *Secret:* You suspect Oberon. You have no proof.
- *Secret:* If forced to choose between Queen and oath-brother, you have already chosen wrong once and will choose wrong again.

Give every player goals that **conflict with at least two other players**. That's the engine of the evening.

## 11. Session Zero — The Relationship Mixer

Before Act I begins, players need both a sense of who they are and a sense of who they know. The Relationship Mixer takes about thirty minutes and produces both. Run it after costumes, after the rules walkthrough, after the briefs are read in private — but **before** any in-character play begins.

### Phase 1 — Court Introductions (≈ 10 min)

Group players physically by court:
- Storms in one corner with Aurora
- Beasts in another with Spriggan
- Mirrors in a third with Mab
- The Knights together (Barghest, Mélusine, Baobhan Sith) plus their personal names

Oberon stands a little apart, alone — as he should, all evening.

Each Sovereign briefly introduces their court to itself: who's who, who serves whom, what the court is publicly working toward tonight (the public goals — **not** the court storyline secret unless the Sovereign chooses to share). Each court member answers with their name, manner, and a one-line self-description. The Knights do the same among themselves.

### Phase 2 — The Mix (≈ 10 min)

The storyteller forms randomized cross-court groups of three or four. Best if no two members of the same court share a group. Each group has roughly five minutes to introduce themselves to one another in character — name, court, one detail.

After five minutes, re-shuffle into fresh groups. By the end of phase 2, most players should have met most other players.

Oberon participates in the mix. This is his first chance to charm everyone in person; he should aim to spend at least a minute or two with everyone.

### Phase 3 — Relationship Selection (≈ 10 min)

Each player picks three other players for relationships. Picks may come from anywhere in the room — not just from the mix-groups; the mixer was for exposure.

The three slots:
- **Strong positive** — trust, best-friend, love, admiration. The deepest bond.
- **Positive** — friend, respected peer, ally, mentor or mentee.
- **Negative** — rivalry, friction, hate, suspicion, old wound.

When you choose someone, walk over to them and propose the slot. The other player must agree before it locks. Together, the two of you write a one-line history on a card or in the brief sheet — *"best friends since we were both pages of Aurora"*, *"old rivals over a stolen mirror, never forgiven"*, *"I love them; they don't quite know."*

### Rules of the mixer

- **Pre-set slots take priority.** Named characters arrive at the mixer with one or more slots already assigned by plot (see *Character_Briefs.md* — Aurora and Mab fill each other's negative, Spriggan also fills Mab as her negative, Barghest and Mélusine fill each other's strong-positive, etc.). Those slots lock first. Fill remaining slots freely.
- **A player may be chosen by at most three others total.** This keeps Sovereigns and Knights from saturating every relationship card. If a player is already at three picks, pick someone else.
- **A player must be chosen by at least one other.** No one stands relationship-less. The storyteller monitors and nudges where needed.
- **Asymmetric relationships are allowed only by mutual agreement.** "I trust them; they're indifferent to me" can be powerful — but both players must consent.

### After the mixer

By the end of phase 3, every player has:
- An assigned Secret Goal (named characters) or a drawn Secret Mission (court members)
- Three relationships, slotted as strong-positive / positive / negative
- A clear sense of who they know and how
- Five to ten minutes of in-character conversation already under their belt with most of the room

Act I can begin.

## 12. Pre-Game Prep Checklist

- [ ] Cast Oberon privately, 1+ week ahead. Brief them on the secret sheet and the three plants.
- [ ] Write character sheets: stats, abilities, public goals, secret goals. One page per character.
- [ ] Print and cut the Secret Mission cards. One hat for the unnamed court members to draw from.
- [ ] Plan the Relationship Mixer (§11). Reserve 30 minutes between briefing and Act I. Identify a storyteller to monitor pick-distribution during phase 3.
- [ ] Print and distribute sheets at least 3 days before the event so players have time to think.
- [ ] Prepare the play space with at least three distinct "rooms" (great hall, private chamber, garden/balcony) for Act III intrigue.
- [ ] Choose a clear act-change signal (a bell, a horn, a song change).
- [ ] Prepare Oberon's costume swap so the reveal is visibly arresting.
- [ ] Recruit at least one Storyteller per 6 players to adjudicate conflicts.
- [ ] Hold a 20-minute rules walkthrough before Act I begins — practice one RPS conflict aloud.
- [ ] Plan a debrief: 15 minutes after the final tableau, sitting in a circle, in voice as players (not characters), to decompress and trade highlights.

## 13. Optional Add-Ons

- **The Fourth Court (Court of the Lake):** If you grow past 15 players or run a sequel, add a fourth city — a water-court led by an unnamed Sovereign Mab dislikes. Mélusine has roots there.
- **Iron Tokens:** Physical objects (a copper ring, a thorn) representing oaths sworn. Returning the token releases the oath. Useful tactile reminder for binding bargains.
- **The Queen's Doom:** A second-evening sequel in which Britain reels from Vortigern. The Knights, scattered, must decide whether a Queen still rules.
