# Relationship Mixer — Facilitator Script
### For the storyteller running session zero of *The Queen's Revel*

**Total runtime:** 30 minutes. **Cast:** in costume, briefs read, rules walked through. **Materials:** a watch or phone timer, a printed list of pre-set relationship slots, a notepad, optional: a small bell or chime for transitions.

---

## Before you begin (≈ 2 min)

Gather everyone standing. Hold up the printed pre-set list and say something like:

> *"Six of you already have one relationship slot assigned by your brief. We'll lock those first. Anyone who's been told their slot has a pre-set partner — Aurora, Mab, Spriggan, Barghest, Mélusine, Baobhan Sith — raise a hand. Good. The rest of you have all three slots free, plus your drawn Secret Mission."*

Confirm the pre-sets aloud so the room knows:
- **Aurora ↔ Mab** — negative slot, each other
- **Spriggan → Mab** — negative slot (Mab gets locked twice; this is correct)
- **Barghest ↔ Mélusine** — strong positive slot, each other
- **Baobhan Sith** — negative slot guided privately (talk to her before the mixer)

---

## Phase 1 — Court Introductions (10 min)

Send players to their court corners. Set a 10-minute timer.

**Opening line (paraphrase):**
> *"For the next ten minutes, you are with your court. Sovereigns, introduce your court to itself. Court members, answer with your fae name, your manner, and one line about what you bring to this Revel. Knights, you're a court of three — same rules. Oberon, you're alone — wander among the courts as a guest, gentle, careful. Don't lock any relationships yet."*

**While they talk, watch for:**
- Players who haven't spoken at all by minute 5 — drift over, ask their name and rank, pull them in.
- Sovereigns dominating — quietly remind them their court members need to speak too.
- Oberon's player slipping into "charming wanderer" mode; the new framing is *kindly shepherd of the dying*. Redirect them if needed.

Call time at 10 minutes.

---

## Phase 2 — The Mix (10 min)

Quickly form randomized cross-court groups of 3 or 4. Easiest method: count off by 3 or 4 within each court, then group all the 1s, all the 2s, etc. Aim for no two players from the same court in any group.

**Opening line:**
> *"You're now in mix groups. Five minutes. Introduce yourselves in character — fae name, court, one detail. The detail should not be your goals or secrets. Make it small: what you wore the day you arrived, what your favorite song is, what your court eats at midwinter. You're meeting strangers."*

At the 5-minute mark, reshuffle into new groups (count off again, mix differently this time). Same rules. Five more minutes.

**While they talk, watch for:**
- Players who clearly aren't clicking — note who looks bored. They'll need help in phase 3.
- Players who are clicking really well — note this. They'll likely choose each other in phase 3.
- Oberon — he should aim to spend at least a minute with everyone. If he's monopolizing one group, move him.

Call time at 10 minutes.

---

## Phase 3 — Relationship Selection (10 min)

Gather everyone in the open room. State the rules clearly:

**Opening line:**
> *"Ten minutes. Each of you picks three other players for three relationships: strong positive, positive, negative. Walk over to them and propose the slot. They must agree. Negotiate the specifics together — write a one-line shared history on your brief. Pre-set slots lock first; if your brief says one slot is pre-set, find that partner now and confirm it before doing anything else. After that, free choice."*

Add the soft rules:
> *"Three caps: no one is picked more than three times total. No one ends with zero. If you find someone already at three, pick someone else. If someone's stuck without picks, I'll come find them a partner."*

**Watch for and intervene on:**
- **Sovereigns and Knights filling everyone's cards.** Stand near the room and call out: *"Mab is at three — pick somewhere else."*
- **Sprites being overlooked.** Note who has no relationship by minute 5. Walk over: *"You — pick someone. And [other quiet player] — pick each other if you click."*
- **Pre-sets not getting locked.** Roam early. Ask Aurora and Mab privately at minute 2: *"Have you two locked your negative slot yet?"*
- **Asymmetric proposals.** If someone offers "I trust them, they're indifferent to me" — confirm both players agree. If yes, fine. If no, ask the proposer to revise.
- **Players negotiating relationships that contradict their secret goal/mission.** If, e.g., a player whose Secret Mission is to expose a Sovereign tries to lock a *strong positive* with them, gently suggest they reconsider (or accept it — the friction can be productive).

Call a soft warning at 8 minutes: *"Two minutes. Lock your remaining slots."*

Call time at 10 minutes.

---

## After the mixer (≈ 2 min)

Quick room sweep:
- Confirm every player has three relationships filled in.
- Confirm everyone with a drawn Secret Mission has it noted.
- Confirm the pre-sets are written down on both sides of each pairing.

Then:
> *"You now know who you are, what you want, and who you know. In two minutes, Act I begins. The Knights will welcome you at the gate. Step back into character. The Queen's empty seat will be the first thing you see."*

Let them settle. Start the bell or chime for Act I.

---

## Troubleshooting cheatsheet

| Problem | Fix |
|---|---|
| Player frozen, won't approach anyone | Pair them with a confident player. "You two — strong positive. Negotiate together." |
| Two players keep gravitating only to each other | Let them lock one slot, then split them up for the other two. |
| Sovereign trying to lock all court members as strong positives | Cap one. The rest can be positives or negatives. |
| Oberon's player slipping back into "flirtatious wanderer" framing | Quiet word: kindly shepherd of the dying, not charming rogue. The reveal lands harder this way. |
| Someone's drawn Secret Mission clearly collides with everyone they like | Quiet re-draw. Don't announce; just hand them a new card. |
| Phase 3 running long | Call the 8-minute warning louder, then enforce the 10-minute close. Any unlocked slots become storyteller-assigned during Act I. |
